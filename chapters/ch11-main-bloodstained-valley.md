# Map 11-M — Bloodstained Valley

[← Home](../index.md)

## Map

![Map 11-M — Bloodstained Valley](images/ch11-main-bloodstained-valley.png)

## Tactics Requirements

- 1 Point: Clear the map.
- 2 Points: Delay your escape to Turn 23.
- 3 Points: Delay your escape to Turn 24.

## Required Deployments for Sidequests

Ward

## Objective Checklist

- Defend the exit point for 20 turns, then have everyone escape through it by Turn 24.
- Secured all 3 tactics points by escaping with everyone on Turn 24.
- Completed Sidequest 1 by letting Ward talk to Rodin (Rodin is in the bottom-left forest)
- Completed Sidequest 2 by letting Morton and Aleck leave the map alive (Morton and Aleck spawns on Turns 11 and 17, respectively)
- Crafting Material: Meteoric Iron (search the center plateau)
- Crafting Material: Platinum (kill the Raze Priest that spawns on Turn 2)
- Crafting Material: Ruby (kill Andersen, he spawns on Turn 10)

## Notes on the Map

The tactics requirements may seem weird to you - but it will make sense soon enough. We start on the right side of the map and must make sure no enemies (except two specific ones - more on that later) make it through. A single enemy slipping past our defenses will result in a game over. After Turn 20, the point we are holding turns into an Escape point, and we just need to get everyone out.

While it feels like we just need to turtle at the defend point until time is up, we are incentivized to proactively push left to complete our sidequests. Thankfully, the sidequests on this map are fairly easy, and once all our jobs are done, we can turtle to our heart's content.

Despite the enemies reportedly being Imperial Elites, the starting enemies on the map are extremely weak cannon fodder. At this stage in the game, enemies with pitiful equipment and stats like those leading the charge are hardly a threat - they will barely be able to land a hit on our army, which seem like superhumans compared to them now. Don't get complacent - the map's enemy quality will steadily ramp up over time. Dispatch the initial goons and take this tempo to make a push west and get our errands done. Near the starting plateau, a Searcher can interact with the sparkling tile to get the crafting material Meteoric Iron, so make a quick stop along your push to grab it.

The bottom-left forest contains the hunter Rodin - hopefully you remember him from Map 3-M. He's back at his usual hunting shenanigans, and just like 3-M, we must find him hidden in the trees and have Ward talk to him to complete the sidequest. Similar to our previous encounter, Rodin will spend his time moving from tree to tree, so find and get him out early.

On Turn 2, a large number of wyvern riders will fly in, led by Zephyrus and Arcturus - those following along in the story will know them already, and more importantly - they're not necessarily our enemies. The squad will sit in the left side of the map and watch us fight for a while. Be warned - **this squad has active enemy AI and will mobilize if we get too close**. Not only is this squad extremely deadly, but fighting them is futile - literally. Zephyrus and Arcturus both have a hidden flag that prevents them from being killed - if a lethal hit were to connect against them, even at 100% accuracy, they would dodge it. The existence of this squad is to prevent you from going too far left and spawn-camping the enemies as they come in.

Suffice it to say, aggroing a massive wyvern squad led by two extremely overpowered boss characters that can't even be killed is a definite death sentence for the map, so stay away from them at all costs. A good example of this is the Raze Priest that also spawns in on the same turn - he is carrying the crafting material Platinum and will drop it on death. Wait for him to leave the wyverns' protection before killing him to avoid running afoul of Zephyrus' army.

After grabbing Rodin and the Platinum, start pulling back a bit - the next notable enemy does not spawn until Turn 10 - the boss of the map, Andersen, will arrive. Andersen is worth killing since he will drop the crafting material Ruby. Just like with the Raze Priest, let him advance a bit before getting the kill. Note that Andersen will advance to about halfway up to the map, and then stop moving, so don't try to draw him out too far - pounce on him as soon it is safe and get out after the kill.

On Turns 11 and 17, the targets for our second sidequest will appear. Morton and Aleck are two characters that we must let through our defensive lines so that they can desert the Empire. The problem is - the game does not label them as "Morton" or "Aleck", or make any efforts to distinguish them from any of the enemies that will give us a game over if they get through. Your only hint to their identity is that these two will not actively attempt to attack you even if they walk up next to you, and if you accidentally kill them, they will say a unique line of dialogue before dying, posthumously informing you of your blunder.

Morton is the bottom-most enemy unit that appears on Turn 11, and Aleck will be the top-most cavalry on Turn 17. One thing that you can use to distinguish them is to check their equipment - in their respective turns that they spawn in on, they are the only ones that equip a Longspear. You can track their locations by pressing START on your controller to mark their tile with a flag, and then continuously replacing the flag as they move forward, make sure to not lose them in the chaos. Let both of them through your defensive point and have them leave the map - you will not game over, and they will play a line of dialogue confirming their identity after getting out successfully.

During this time, with all your errands done, it's in your best interest to start pulling back to your starting point and hunker down in anticipation for the end of the defense phase.

On Turn 19, a massive wave of enemies will appear in the west - lance cavalry, horse archers, and a Raze Priest carrying a Wormwood siege orb. This squad will run you over without a second thought and will immediately begin their march. This squad - which I can dub as the "Kill Team", will appear and herald the end of the first half of the map.

Turn 20 afterward will signal the end of the defense phase. Zephyrus and Arcturus will pull their army away, and the defend point changes into an escape point. Because there is no longer a place to defend, enemies will shift their AI from trying to get out of the map to trying to exclusively kill you. While you can on paper just flee the map immediately and win, maximum tactics points demand us stay behind for the longest possible time and get out only on Turn 24. You are therefore expected to hunker down and engage in combat with the aforementioned "Kill Team" until it is time to flee - at least, that's likely what Kaga intended you to do.

Yeah, we're not doing that. The end of this map can be cheesed fairly easily. Because the enemies have shifted their AI to attack you, you can have all but 1 unit flee, and make sure the one person you leave behind has the "Hide" passive skill. Put them in a forest right next to the escape point and wait (or activate a Vanishstone if the unit you left behind cannot Hide). Since the only playable unit on the map is hidden, and none of the enemies have Watchful, the enemies on this map will not detect a valid target to attack and stand still forever. Simply spam end turn until Turn 24 arrives and take a brisk stroll out of the map with your hidden unit, ending the map without needing to engage with the Kill Team.

Overall, this is an incredibly easy and comfy defense map, especially if you choose to cheese the Kill Team at the end and abuse the fact that they're all shortsighted.
