# Map 9-M — Breaking Bridges

[← Home](../index.md)

## Map

![Map 9-M — Breaking Bridges](images/ch09-main-breaking-bridges.png)

## Tactics Requirements

- 1 Point: Clear the map.
- 2 Points: At least 4 engineers survive. (excluding the Head Engineer)
- 3 Points: All 5 engineers survive. (excluding the Head Engineer)

## Required Deployments for Sidequests

Reese, Adel, *Axel**, *Arthur***, *Esteban****

* Axel is not required for a sidequest, deploying Axel is required for his permanent recruitment, which unlocks if you have viewed his town event right before this map, unlocked at 5+ Happiness.

** Arthur is not required for a sidequest, deploying Arthur is required for his happiness ending. If he is not permanently recruited, he will permanently recruit himself in Chapter 10 with 1+ Happiness (which he can get here if you have never used him for some reason)

*** I blatantly lied here: Esteban is **not even required on this map**. Esteban will be permanently recruited after this map if he has 5+ Happiness, But he does **not** need to join this map, he can be brought along to make an event on this map significantly easier to complete. If you complete the event the harder way without him deployed, **it still counts**.

## Objective Checklist

- Have Reese speak to the Head Engineer.
- Secured Axel's recruitment by visiting the left house (Axel requires 5+ Happiness)
- Secured Esteban's recruitment by defeating Sophie or have Esteban force her surrender (Sophie spawns on Turn 2, Esteban requires 5+ Happiness, Esteban does not necessarily need to be deployed)
- Secured Arthur's happy ending by visiting the bottom-right most house
- Completed Sidequest 1 by visiting the southern house with Adel
- Completed Sidequest 2 by having Reese wait next to the spy (the spy is the enemy archer in the top-right corner)
- Completed Sidequest 3 by procuring 3 Imperial Spears (you potentially already have this done from all the previous maps)
- Crafting Material: Platinum (kill the lancer in the western island)
- Crafting Material: Nottos Iron (search the house in the bottom-right side of the map)
- Crafting Material: Tiger Pelt (investigate the bag to the right of Fort Remagen)
- Gallery Item: Sleep (kill the Raze Priest in the second reinforcing wave, spawns on Turn 21 or potentially earlier if the Courier survived)
- Notable Loot: 10,000 Denarii (open the treasure chest in the Fort prison)
- Notable Loot: Xyston (kill Ozama)

## Notes on the Map

Behold the splendor that is Remagen Bridge, take one long look at it - after we're done here, this bridge will be gone and the Kingdom of Veria will live to see another day. For now, this bridge is filled with some of the most terrifying enemies this game will ever expect you to fight. No longer are we dealing with petty bandits or some small advance force from the Empire. This is the first time we will be getting a glimpse of the full might of an Imperial army - but I'm getting ahead of myself. This map is filled to the brim with things to do, it's hard to sort it all out in an organized way, so let's try to break it down starting with the map layout.

Our goal is to reach Remagen Fort and free the Engineers, then have Reese speak to the Head Engineer. There are two ways into the fort - you can go from the western island and lower the drawbridge from across the water, or go the long way and cross Remagen Bridge itself to reach the fort. Though this map doesn't appear to be timed, there *is* still a soft time limit - a single archer will be shooting at all the imprisoned Engineers, slowly killing them. It takes this archer anywhere between 4-5 hits to kill an Engineer, but he is the world's laziest executioner. This archer never moves, and because the Engineer NPCs shuffle around in the prison, the archer will be shooting a different person each turn, depending on which unlucky engineer shuffled into his attack range on that turn. To secure maximum tactics, we need to get into the fort before the archer kills someone - a surprisingly lenient time constraint, since the archer can't seem to make up his mind on who to kill - but it is a disincentive for us to go glacially slow.

Let's start with the long way through the bridge for better narrative flow. On Turn 1, a courier will spot us and immediately flee top-right to call for reinforcements. If the courier reaches his destination, he will trigger reinforcements to appear early - if this courier is intercepted and killed, then those reinforcements will be delayed to Turn 20. The early stages of this map is quite hectic, so we really don't want to face the reinforcements until later. It is in your best interest to go for the Turn 20 delay, so chase this courier down and murder him.

Along the way, you'll find a soldier that drops his Imperial Spear on death. There is a sidequest given to us to procure 3 of these spears - but this quest doesn't specify the spears have to be from this map. Throughout our entire game thus far, we've most certainly captured quite a few enemies already. In all likelihood, you *already* have 3 Imperial Spears to complete the sidequest with just from all the previous enemies we've taken in, since "Imperial Spear" is such a common weapon. If you don't, though, this map has exactly 3 enemies that have spears, and two of them drop it on death - the first enemy is right in front of you, the second enemy is past the bridge, and the third enemy is in the prison fort standing on the treasure chest. The third enemy on the chest must be captured to take his spear, the other two will drop it.

Check your item list prior to starting this mission - see how many Imperial Spears you already have. If you already have enough to fulfill the sidequest, you can turn those in after this map and not have to worry about it here. If you are missing some, this map offers you the chance to take what you need to fill the quota.

There are a few houses along the way to the bridge while chasing the courier. Adel can investigate the house right before the bridge to complete a sidequest. Two other houses can be searched for 1000 Denarii and the crafting material Nottos Iron after a fight. The last one is the bottom-right most house on the cliff. Arthur can investigate it to trigger a cutscene that will lead to his personal happiness ending - we all like Arthur, so let's give him a happy ending!

Horse units sprinting to intercept the courier full-speed will manage to kill him through his beefy HP pool right before the bridge - just in time to tackle the four horsemen of the apocalypse. The bridge is guarded by four named Lance Cavalry units - Ozama, Marvell, Simon, and Gardy. They have two ballistae and two horse archers behind them for ranged support as well. Triggering combat against this group will rapidly spell disaster unless prepared. We've fought one lancer in the past - Theodore in 6-M, but starting from this map, they will show up in large numbers, so let's take some time to dissect these units.

Lancers are terrifying. It is worth noting that in *Berwick Saga*, lances fall into the class of spears and relies on the weapon rank of spears, they behave completely differently. Lances have a bidirectional Windsweep effect - which is to say that units cannot counterattack against a lance hit in any circumstance, but lances also cannot counterattack you back if you initiate combat. Lances also carry extreme amounts of momentum damage - they gain anywhere between 3 to 4 additional points of damage for every hex traveled prior to initiating combat. If a Lancer runs at you from 8 tiles away, the momentum damage stacks on top of their base stats to deliver over 30+ damage - occasionally 40+ - in a single hit, this is enough to kill just about anyone outright. With four lancers charging you down, this squad can wipe your army in the blink of an eye if you are unprepared. The most nightmarish part is that lancers tend to also have very high defense and very powerful shields, making landing damage against them difficult.

There are two notable ways to beat Lancers - the first method is to play very proactively and get in their face. If they are not moving prior to making their attack, then their momentum damage are minimized to greatly dull their damage output. You do want to make this hit count, though. Because lancers cannot receive counterattacks, if they attack you at point-blank range, it will allow them to unconditionally canto far away, causing you to go on a wild goose chase, potentially forcing you to overextend - or get into a weird push and pull game of engaging and disengaging. Lancers are tanky enough one hit is usually not enough to kill them, and require some form of attrition to wear them down while staying safe.

The second way, and a much cleaner way to neutralize the threat - is to just kill their horse. Broadhead Arrows and Poleaxes are wonderful weapons for being able to instantly neutralize them. Lances **cannot** be used while dismounted. So if a lancer loses their horse, they're completely useless. Note that some Lancers later in the game carry a backup spear to still be useful in the event their horse dies, but all 8 lancers on this map *only* have lances and nothing else. If a lancer's horse dies, the terrifying power of their lance dies with them, and a lot of lancers will change their AI to flee the map if their horse is dead.

Of course, there is a *third* way - which is just throwing Larentia at them. Because lances cannot counterattack and are 0 range, Larentia cannot be targeted or retaliated by a lancer in any circumstance. Her damage output is often times not enough to do meaningful damage to the high defense of lancers, but it offers a completely safe avenue to slowly dismantle them over time.

With that general description out of the way - let's take a look at this kill squad on the bridge. Ozama, Marvell, Simon, and Gardy all have the Supporter skill, giving each other support bonuses which increases their accuracy - if all four of them run at you, they will gradually add Supporter bonuses in each other's proximity and score increasingly accurate hits with crazy damage output. Pushing into them is risky since you will be tanking two ballistae and two horse archers that have high-power arrows. It is in your best interest to draw the lancer squad away from their ranged support and kill them, blitzing them down as fast as possible to avoid them cantoing back across the bridge into ranged support. The leader of this squad, Ozama, will drop his lance on death. Some of our cavalry units can use lances on promotion, but Clifford can notably use them at base.

This bridge is terrifying - especially if this is your first time fighting lancer squads. Some brute force of extreme kiting back is required. The ranged support can eventually be neutralized and the ballistae can be taken down with speedy fliers or Arrowbane, but your biggest obstacle will be the Lancers. Once you're past this bridge, the fort is just a small walk away. We'll talk about the fort later, but let's turn our attention back to the approach by talking about the other route now.

The western shoals are also guarded by four lancers, but they're significantly less scary - not only do they not have the named enemies' skills on the bridge, but they also have much less room to maneuver around with on the tiny island. One of the lancers here drops the crafting material Platinum on death, so make sure to grab it off him. The house on the western island can be investigated with Axel - this secures his permanent recruitment after a battle against the resident inside. Past this small island is a drawbridge with a crossbow guarding it. Killing the crossbow will allow you to swim or fly across to lower the bridge and make the shortcut into the fort happen.

Be warned - this crossbowman has a Chu-Ko-Nu for quadruple attacks, *and* has Sleep Arrows, *and* has Counter to hit back against attacks made at melee range. Being caught off guard will cause you to eat a large volley of bolts in return and be put to sleep in a terrible location. You can try to snipe him from range across the bridge, but he is covered by a Raze Priest in Remagen Fort, who will reach and heal him with Darkmend. Make sure the priest's action has been taken, chip him down, then rush him with a melee hit to clear him off the drawbridge controls, lower the bridge and we're through!

Whichever route you took to get to the fort (perhaps even both at the same time), there are two threats waiting for us - first is the Gigas Knight near the fort entrance. Gigas Knights are super-armor knights. They have very high offensive power and insurmountable defenses. Being smacked by their behemoth of an axe once is often enough to outright kill our squishy units. They are susceptible to anti-armor tools like hammers, and as always, generally melt to any form of magical damage. Put a mage in his face and delete him - but keep in mind that most Gigas Knights have counter, including this one, so be wary of eating reprisal hits if you're fighting at melee. This particular Gigas Knight also has a Thunder Ring, giving him a decent chunk of Thunder resistance - Percy's thunder isn't a good candidate, but Enid (or Aegina/Owen with secondary fire access) can delete this man with Fire since the Thunder Ring pushes his Fire resistance into the negatives.

The second threat is the named bow knight Sophie. If you've been following the story, this is not the first time you've seen her, and she's someone that somehow became entangled with Esteban following their initial meeting. She will appear on Turn 2 and sit beside the boss, but will ride out and join the fight once the door to the fort opens. Sophie is equipped with the Prf bow Pascanion - a "Devil" bow that carries a very high might, but has a backfire rate inversely scaling with bow rank. Her ember arrows and the high Mt of the Pascanion means and she cleanly two-shots almost everyone in your army, and her bow rank of 30 means that the Pascanion only has a 20% backfire rate on her - it can happen, but shouldn't be relied on.

We must defeat Sophie to proceed deeper into the fort. Defeating Sophie will allow us to take her captive (via story - she has Robust so you can't actually capture her), and trigger an event after the map which eventually leads to Esteban's recruitment. Taking her HP to 0 is fairly easy once you actually reach her, but taking a few shots from the Pascanion is always risky. The alternative, easier way is to just have Esteban attack her. This will trigger a dialogue and Sophie will immediately surrender, defeating her without combat.

If Esteban is not deployed, this doesn't stop him from being recruited, Sophie will be taken captive with or without him in the map. Deploying Esteban anyway is a fantastic way to unlock the alternative avenue of forcing her surrender without combat! Given the way into the fort is so constrained, it may take us some shuffling around to get him there, but it is a much easier alternative if you can make it happen.

Once inside the fort, you can actually defuse the time threat entirely. Standing on the cliffs in the outer fort allows us to reach the archer shooting at the engineers from 2-range and duel him with a ranged unit of our own. He's actually not that strong, and killing the executioner will save the engineers forever - the prison never attempts to send a second archer to attack the imprisoned engineers, and it allows us to take the rest of the map slowly. Be careful to not open the final door into the boss room - this activates a map trigger to open the prison cells for the engineers as well, and they'll happily swarm out to get themselves killed. Simply slay the executioner over the wall and start mopping up the rest of the map.

To the right of Fort Remagen, there is a bag that we can investigate to get the crafting material Tiger Pelt. The top-right corner of the map also features an inconspicuous archer. This archer is the Narvian spy and speaking to him is required for a sidequest - **do not kill him**. This archer has Mercy and will attack us with Mercy active as we approach, so he can never kill us. Note that Reese *does not* have a Talk option with the spy, simply have Reese *wait* next to the spy to complete the sidequest and the spy will leave afterwards.

With the executioner dead, feel free to go around the map now searching the houses and investigating stuff that you may not have had time for before, bide your time until Turn 20 - the courier's death delayed their arrival, but they will arrive nonetheless. Three enemies spawn per turn in the top-right for two turns, and these six enemies represent all of the reinforcements on the entire map. The second wave notably has a Raze Priest that drops his Sleep Orb on death. **This is a gallery item for Erzheimer**, but you may have already had one already from killing Arzovy in X-4, if Faye's paralogue was already done. If not, take it here.

With the entire map cleared, it's time to wrap up this map by opening the inner fort. Excluding the executioner, there are only three enemies remaining - an archer, a soldier that carries the third Imperial Spear, and the boss Grauve. Grauve is carrying a Black Shield - a shield with a 33% chance to reflect damage back to its attacker. Rest assured - this won't be the last time you see this annoying shield. You can attempt to break it with Breaker Arrows or similar anti-shields, but Black Shields also have very low durability, breaking after blocking only a few hits.

You may be tempted to nuke Grauve with magic - don't. He has Magicbane. You also technically don't have to kill him, since the objective isn't to rout or defeat boss. We are nonetheless heavily incentivized to do so: He will get in your way and the now-freed Engineers will be roaming around, and there is still a treasure chest in the fort that we have to unlock to get a hefty sum of 10,000 Denarii and potentially the third Imperial Spear to capture if you're missing it, so removing Grauve does make our life easy. Break through his shield and take him down, tidy up the remaining enemies, then bring Reese to speak with the Head Engineer and we are finally done.

Note that just like in *Thracia 776*, mounted units must dismount before going indoors. *Berwick Saga* mostly doesn't deal with this because there are very few indoor areas in this game, this map's prison cell is a small patch of indoor ground, so Reese does have to respect the law and leave his horse at the door if he's going in to talk to the Head Engineer.
