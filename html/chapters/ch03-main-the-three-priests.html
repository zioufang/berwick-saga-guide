Map 3-M — The Three Priests

← Home

Map

Tactics Requirements

  • 1 Point: Clear the map.
  • 2 Points: At least 2 priests survive.
  • 3 Points: All 3 priests survive.

Required Deployments for Sidequests

Ward, Axel, Christine, Faye*

  • Faye is not necessarily required, but makes your life slightly easier and she will get an unique item for doing it.

Objective Checklist

  • Have all the Priests make it to the end, then have Reese arrive at the temple.
  • All 3 tactics points secured by keeping all the priests alive.
  • 1x Antitoxin (searched in house on left plateau)
  • 1x Treasure (searched in middle pirate fort)
  • 20x Bronze Arrows+ (searched in house in bottom-right)
  • Crafting Material: Eye of Balor (searched in top-left cave)
  • Crafting Material: Gale Shard (dropped by Turn 14 reinforcing bandit)
  • Completed Sidequest 1 by searching the lake for the mirror with Axel
  • Completed Sidequest 2 by having Ward speak to Rodin in the eastern plateau
  • Completed Sidequest 3 by having Christine speak to Esteban on Turn 20
  • New Gallery Item: Darkmend (dropped by Fermat, will complete Sidequest 4)
  • New Gallery Item: Assassin's Knife (acquired by capturing Garos, optional)
  • New Bounty Target: Agvan (map boss)
  • New Bounty Target: Garos (appears on Turn 20)

Notes on the Map

Alright. Let's do this.

This map has some weird degree of infamy in Berwick Saga, particularly around the audience that haven't actually played the game. It's brought up a lot as an example of Kaga's borderline-sadistic map design tendencies. The map is split into three lanes, with a temple at the end. All three priests have decided on their own routes to take, and will converge at the same endpoint. Along the way, the three priests will each run into their share of danger, and we must split our army three ways to protect them. This is therefore a triple-escort mission, how fun.

Each of the three routes contain a house at approximately the halfway point, and each of these houses have a distinct event that will occur. We'll get to those. It makes the most sense to divide the explanation by routes, and explain each individually. Keep in mind of which sidequest occurs on which route, to properly divide your army.

This map might appear as if it's timed - since the priests will march onward to the temple, even if there are several dozen bloodthirsty enemies in their face - but it's actually not. Priests have 3 movement, and all carry some form of healing on them. They won't be shy about lending you a heal here or there if you happen to have injured units next to them. The priests can also be bodyblocked along their route, causing them to get stuck - but I don't recommend doing this, it doesn't really do anything productive. Once all the priests arrive at the temple, you are allowed to stay on the map as long as you want afteward, as well, since the map won't end until Reese reaches the temple.

The Top Route: The top lane is taken by the priest Grimm, and is probably the easiest of the three routes. Initially, there will be some hidden thieves and archers in the starting forests, but afterwards, he has a relatively clear path to the temple.

To the right of the top lane is a small plateau with two archers. One of these archers has poison arrows, the other one has the Triple-Shot Skill. These enemies will be fairly annoying - especially if they land a poison hit on Grimm, but you can ascend the plateau ahead of time to fight them. If you do get poisoned, search the house on that plateau to find an antitoxin to cure it off.

The top-left cave can also be searched for the Eye of Balor crafting material after a fight. This cave will also eventually see archer and axe bandit reinforcements spawning in. The archers will pepper Grimm from high ground, doing minimal chip damage to him - this for the most part isn't problematic. Grimm will take 4 damage from each archer shot that connects him from that reinforcing cave.

Along the way, Grimm will stop by the church and meet the character Owen. Owen will be a playable character starting in Chapter 4! After this, it's a clean stroll for Grimm to the exit.

The Middle Route: The middle lane is taken by the priest Orbel. Orbel is armed with a Miracle Charm, which prevents one instance of fatal damage - this is important.

Orbel's route is a narrow 1-hex route, with the aforementioned archer plateau on his left and a lake on the right. The lake has a shimmering spot in the middle, which Axel must travel to and search it to grab the mirror - this will complete one of the sidequests. The fort to the right of this river will spawn some decently powerful pirate reinforcements. After Axel grabs the mirror, stand on this fort to search for the treasure, then live there and block the reinforcements. Aquatic units aren't the worst, but due to our inability to easily cross water, a few of them harassing Orbel is a fantastic way to get him killed, so make sure to block the fort to stop the problem.

Along the way to the temple, Orbel will stop by the house in the middle and trigger a cutscene. This cutscene will cause him to enter combat and receive 15 HP of damage. This is unavoidable, it's also why he has a Miracle Charm to hopefully prevent death. If Orbel has less than 15 HP before entering this house, he will be forced to break the charm. If the charm was already broken and he enters the house with less than 15 HP, he will die. Make an effort to protect him on his way to this house, and ensure he survives it.

After this jumpscare, Orbel should be cleared to reach the temple and clear his route.

The Bottom Route: The bottom lane has the most going on. The priest Sahkalin will take this route to the temple. Along the way, he will be harassed by a lot of bandits on the eastern plateau, but this route is filled with sidequests that require units like Ward and Faye to do.

Before ascending the plateau, the bottom-right most house can be searched for a bundle of Bronze Arrows+, but this can be saved for later when the map settles down. The plateau itself features the NPC Rodin. Rodin has the Hide ability, and will spend his time moving from tree to tree in concealment. You can detect him using a unit with Watchful, or just accidentally bumping into him by stopping adjacent to him somewhere along the way. Have Ward speak with Rodin to clear a sidequest. Rodin will never be attacked or die, so you don't have to worry about his safety.

The bigger issue is the cutscene on Turn 2 - on the eastern house that Sakhalin will eventually visit, two enemies will spawn there. The Dark Master Chaos and the Raze Priest Fermat. Chaos is a monstrous being, you will eventually fight Chaos with the stats he is shown here on the final map of the game as one of the final bosses. Needless to say, trying to kill him here would be almost impossible. There will be two variants of this cutscene that can play - one if Faye is alive, and one if Faye is dead.

If Faye is dead, then Chaos will simply just leave, and an alternate cutscene will play when this house is visited by Sakhalin that will eventually lead to Volo's recruitment. If you RNG abuse the hell out of the game and somehow defeat Chaos, then this alternate variant will also play. If not, then Chaos will stay on this house and slice Sakhalin in two effortlessly when he arrives. To get rid of him in the "normal" way, have Faye walk up and speak to him. A cutscene will play where Faye will challenge Chaos to a 1v1, lose, and leave the map briefly, but this also removes Chaos from the map. Once Sakhalin reaches the house, he will heal Faye, give her a free Miracle Charm, and she will then rejoin the map and return under your control. Make sure to do this, or Sakhalin is as good as dead if he bumps into Chaos.

Meanwhile, the Raze Priest Fermat will also roam about, healing enemies with his Darkmend orb. Upon death, he will drop this orb, and turning it into the item collector Erzheimer is one of our sidequests for this chapter. This also kickstarts the Item Collection series of quests.

Killing Fermat to secure his Darkmend will complete the first out of your 18 items required for Erzheimer. Due to the nature of this map, Fermat will roam around a lot healing his wounded allies, and may even stumble into the middle lane to do so. Make sure to get rid of him at some point.

The Boss: All three routes eventually converge to the temple door, where the bandit leader Agvan is waiting. Agvan is on the bounty list, so capturing him yields double rewards. He is notably holding a weapon that he's officially too dumb to use. In Berwick Saga, you are allowed to use weapons that have ranks higher than what you need to properly use them, with every differing level resulting in a 5% chance of failing to swing with the weapon. Agvan's level is 2 lower than the sword he is attempting to use, meaning 10% of the time, he will fail to attack - unfortunately, this probability is too low to be relied upon, but it is nice when it happens. Agvan will sit on the temple door and, much like Bengriton, spam Battle Cry to boost his combat power. But unlike Bengriton, Agvan is fully aggressive and will gladly walk out of the temple door to fight you - don't get caught off guard by this. Wandering too close to him and eating a 6-stack Battle Cry hit from Agvan is almost certain death. Remember to defuse his Battle Cry stacks by either poking him at range, or simply getting the first hit on him and damaging him before he can retaliate. Set up for the capture if you desire, but otherwise just kill him to fulfill the bounty.

With Agvan dead and the priests mostly in the clear, they can be left to slowly waddle over to the temple and reach it. When you are reasonably sure that their pursuers won't reach them, ditch the priests and allow them to wander to the door, and start bringing your entire army toward the east and the south edges of the map. The priests may have arrived, but our job isn't quite done yet on this map.

On Turn 14, a bandit will reinforce from the south side of the map and attempt to flee to the right. He is carrying the crafting material Gale Shard, kill him for it.

Starting on Turn 16 and ending in Turn 18, a few enemy reinforcements will trickle in from the bottom-right corner. These enemies aren't particularly threatening, but make sure to spare some effort in dealing with them. We will have some fighting to do on Turn 20, and we don't want them harassing us.

Speaking of - on Turn 20, two events will happen. First, Esteban will spawn on the eastern side of the map. Christine must speak with Esteban to complete and sidequest, and turn Esteban into a recruitable character. By now, the priests will have definitely left the map - either into the temple or into a body bag, depending on how your map went. So you will have enough time to anticipate his arrival, camp his entrance with Christine and talk to him to get him out. Meanwhile, the southern side of the map will see another enemy arrive - Garos.

Garos is the first enemy to have the skill Obfuscate - it disallows you from checking his stats, and even trying to do combat with him will show a ton of question marks in the forecast. Similar to Hide, Obfuscate can be dispelled with a Watchful unit at 3 tiles, or anyone else within 1 tile. Garos is on the Bounty List. Capturing him will yield double rewards, but it is unfortunately not that simple. Equipped with an Assassin's Knife, a ludicrously broken weapon with 80% critical rate, as well as the ability Counter to always hit back, trying to attack Garos will almost certainly have him crit you in the face in return. In Berwick Saga, critical hits do not triple your damage, but instead add anywhere between 10 to 20 "true damage", which cannot be negated or reduced in any way. Having Garos swiping at any unit one or two times will almost certainly kill them.

To complicate matters even further, Garos is, for the most part, completely uninterested in fighting and will focus on moving right to escape the map. He will stop to attack you if you are trying to block his way, but otherwise he will leave the map in just a few turns of his arrival. You must anticipate his spawn and camp him out, doing your best to kill or capture him. If you want to land the capture, you can try having Sylvis aim at him with her starting Composite Bow and Steel Arrows+, which will have just shy of a 50/50 shot of landing the cripple if Maim activates. Alternatively, brute forcing him with Arthur's Flourish can also work if you are willing to take a few strong hits in return. If all of this setup is too hard, or if Garos' very high evasion bypasses these checks, you can also try to provoke him onto a cliff tile to make him lose all his Avoid and burst him down. If you absolutely cannot get the capture, at least go for the kill.

If you do manage to successfully capture Garos, you will also take his Assassin's Knife, which happens to be another one of the 18 items for Erzheimer's gallery. You don't have to get this knife here, or sweat too much about it. There will be plenty of Assassin's Knives later for us to take in the game, including one that is a guaranteed drop. So just focus on killing Garos if you cannot get the cripple to work.

Finally, with all of this out of the way, Reese can be brought to the temple to bring an end to this extremely hectic map. Just like 2-M, the tactics points will turn out to be the easiest part of this map. Correct assignment of your forces are vital, and so is anticipating where side objectives spawn and setting up beforehand to address it.
